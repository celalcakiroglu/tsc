Discrete Time Signals¶

Examples of signal sources that might be filtered:

aircraft altitude, concentration of a species in a liquid, level of liquid in a tank, flux of a liquid into a tank, pressure, electric current, various vibration measurements, audio signals, pixels of an image, vehicle velocity, air humidity

Discrete - time \((DT)\) signals can be obtained by sampling continuous time signals or they can be discrete in nature. \(DT\) signals are denoted like \(x(n)\) where \(n\) is the sample index. \(n\) usually starts from \(0\) and increases with unit steps \((\Delta n =1)\). If this is not the case, the following transformation can be applied:

Let \(n(k)\) be the sequence of time instants such that for any \(k \in \mathbb{N}\), \(n(k)=n(0)+k\Delta n\), where \(n(0) \neq 0\) and \(\Delta n \neq 1\). We can define a time sequence \({n(k)}'\) which satisfies the requirements of zero initial time and unit time step as follows: Let \({n(k)}'=an(k) + b\), \(k \in \mathbb{N}\) and impose the initial conditions \({n(0)}'=0\), \({n(1)}'=1\). Then we obtain the following two equations:

(1)\[{n(0)}'=an(0)+b \Rightarrow b=-an(0)\]
(2)\[{n(1)}'=an(1)+b \Rightarrow 1=an(1)-an(0)=a\Delta n \Rightarrow a=\frac{1}{\Delta n}, b=-\frac{1}{\Delta n} n(0)\]

Inserting these two equations in the expression for \({n(k)}'\) gives:

\[{n(k)}'=\frac{1}{\Delta n} n(k) -\frac{1}{\Delta n} n(0)= \frac{1}{\Delta n} \Big( n(k)-n(0) \Big) = \frac{1}{\Delta n}k \Delta n = k\]

Power and energy of a signal¶

The power of a DT signal \(x(n)\) is defined as:

\[P_x = \lim_{N\to \infty}\displaystyle\frac{1}{2N+1}\sum_{n=-N}^{N}|x(n)|^2\]

If a signal has finite power, then the signal is called power signal

The energy of a DT signal \(x(n)\) is defined as:

\[E_x = \sum_{n=-\infty}^{\infty}|x(n)|^2\]

If a signal has finite energy, then the signal is called energy signal. The power of an energy signal is equal to zero and the energy of a power signal is infinite.
