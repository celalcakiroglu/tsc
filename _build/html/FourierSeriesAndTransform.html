Fourier Series and the Fourier Transform¶

Fourier Series¶

The Fourier series representation of a periodic discrete-time signal \(x(n)\) is defined as

(1)\[x(n)=\sum_{k=0}^{N-1}c_k e^{j2\pi kn/N}\]

where \(N\) is the fundamental period of \(x(n)\). In equation (1), \(c_k\) denotes the Fourier coefficients of \(x(n)\) and they represent the frequency components contained in \(x(n)\). For each \(k \in \lbrace 0, 1, ..., N-1 \rbrace\), \(c_k\) can be calculated as

(2)\[c_k=\frac{1}{N}\sum_{n=0}^{N-1}x(n)e^{-j2\pi kn/N}\]

Equation (2) can be derived applying the following 4 steps:

  • Step 1: Both sides of equation (1) are multiplied by \(e^{-j2\pi ln/N}\), where \(l \in \lbrace 0, 1, ..., N-1 \rbrace\).
(3)\[\begin{split}x(n) e^{-j2\pi ln/N} & = \sum _{k=0}^{N-1} c_k e^{j 2\pi (k-l) n/N } \\ & = \sum _{k=0}^{N-1} c_k \Big\lbrace e^{j2\pi (k-l)/N}\Big\rbrace^n \\\end{split}\]
  • Step 2: Let \(r=e^{j2\pi (k-l)/N}\) and sum both sides of equation (3) over the index \(n\)
(4)\[\sum _{n=0}^{N-1}x(n)e^{-j2\pi ln/N} = \sum _{k=0}^{N-1} c_k \sum _{n=0}^{N-1} r^n\]
  • Step 3: Using the geometric series summation:
\[\begin{split} \begin{equation*} \sum _{n=0}^{N-1} r^n= \left\{ \begin{array}{ll} N & \mbox{ if $ k=l$}\\ \displaystyle\frac{1-r^N}{1-r} & \mbox{ if $k \neq l$} \end{array} \right. = \left\{ \begin{array}{ll} N & \mbox{ if $ k=l$}\\ \displaystyle\frac{1- e^{j2\pi (k-l)} }{1-e^{j2\pi (k-l)/N}} & \mbox{ if $k \neq l$} \end{array} \right. \end{equation*}\end{split}\]
\[\begin{split}e^{j2\pi (k-l)}=\cos{2\pi (k-l)} + j\sin{2\pi (k-l)}=1 \Rightarrow \sum _{n=0}^{N-1} r^n= \left\{ \begin{array}{ll} N & \mbox{ if $ k=l$}\\ 0 & \mbox{ if $k \neq l$} \end{array} \right.\end{split}\]
  • Step 4: Plugging the result of step 3 in equation (4) we obtain:
\[\sum _{n=0}^{N-1}x(n)e^{-j2\pi ln/N} = c_0 \cdot 0 + c_1 \cdot 0 + ... + c_l \cdot N + c_{l+1}\cdot 0 + ... +c_{N-1}\cdot 0 = c_l \cdot N\]
\[c_l=\frac{1}{N}\sum _{n=0}^{N-1}x(n)e^{-j2\pi ln/N}\]

Example: Determine the Fourier coefficients of the signal \(x(n)=\cos(\frac{2\pi}{3}n)+\sin(\frac{2\pi}{5}n)\).

\[x(n)=\frac{1}{2}e^{j\displaystyle\frac{2\pi}{3}n}+\frac{1}{2}e^{j\Big(-\displaystyle\frac{2\pi}{3}\Big)n}+\frac{1}{2j}e^{j\displaystyle\frac{2\pi}{5}n}-\frac{1}{2j}e^{j\Big(-\displaystyle\frac{2\pi}{5}\Big)n}\]
\[e^{j\Big(-\displaystyle\frac{2\pi}{3}\Big)n}=e^{j\Big(-\displaystyle\frac{2\pi}{3}+2\pi\Big)n}=e^{j\displaystyle\frac{4\pi}{3}n}, \quad e^{j\Big(-\displaystyle\frac{2\pi}{5}\Big)n}=e^{j\Big(-\displaystyle\frac{2\pi}{5}+2\pi\Big)n}=e^{j\displaystyle\frac{8\pi}{5}n}\]
(5)\[\Rightarrow x(n)=\frac{1}{2j}e^{j\displaystyle\frac{2\pi}{5}n}+\frac{1}{2}e^{j\displaystyle\frac{2\pi}{3}n}+\frac{1}{2}e^{j\displaystyle\frac{4\pi}{3}n}-\frac{1}{2j}e^{j\displaystyle\frac{8\pi}{5}n}\]

Let \(x_1(n)=\cos(2\pi\frac{1}{3}n)\), \(x_2(n)=\sin(2\pi\frac{1}{5}n)\). Let \(N_1\) and \(N_2\) be the fundamental periods of \(x_1\) and \(x_2\) respectively. Then \(N_1=3\) and \(N_2=5\). The fundamental period of \(x(n)\) is the smallest common factor of \(N_1\) and \(N_2\) such that \(N=15\). It follows that

(6)\[x(n)=\sum_{k=0}^{14}c_k e^{j\displaystyle\frac{2\pi}{15}kn}\]

The only nonzero terms in (6) are the ones in (5)

\[j\frac{2\pi}{5}n=j\frac{2\pi}{15}3n \Rightarrow k=3, c_3=-j/2 \quad;\quad j\frac{2\pi}{3}n=j\frac{2\pi}{15}5n \Rightarrow k=5, c_5 =1/2\]
\[j\frac{4\pi}{3}n=j\frac{2\pi}{15}10n \Rightarrow k=10, c_{10}=1/2 \quad;\quad j\frac{8\pi}{5}n=j\frac{2\pi}{15}12n \Rightarrow k=12, c_{12} =j/2\]

Power density spectrum of a periodic signal¶

The power of a periodic signal \(x(n)\) with the fundamental period \(N\) can be calculated using the formula

\[P_x=\sum_{k=0}^{N-1} |c_k|^2\]

This formula can be derived from the formula of the power of a general signal in the following steps:

  • Step 1: The power of a general signal \(x(n)\) is defined as
\[P_x=\lim_{N\to\infty}\frac{1}{2N+1}\sum_{n=-N}^{N} |x(n)|^2\]

According to this formula the power of the signal is equal to the average value of \(|x(n)|^2\) over the entire discrete time domain. In case of a periodic signal, since the fundamental period of the signal is repeating itself, it is enough to take the average of \(|x(n)|^2\) over the fundamental period as follows

(7)\[P_x=\frac{1}{N}\sum_{n=0}^{N-1}|x(n)|^2\]
  • Step 2: (7) can be rearranged using the following 2 identities:
\[|x(n)|^2=x(n)\cdot {x(n)}^*\]
(8)\[{x(n)}^*=\sum_{k=0}^{N-1}{c_k}^*e^{-j2\pi kn/N}\]

The derivation of (8) is as follows:

\[\begin{split}x(n) &= \sum_{k=0}^{N-1}c_k e^{j2\pi kn/N } \\ &= \sum _{k=0}^{N-1}c_k\lbrace \cos(\displaystyle\frac{2\pi k }{N}n) + j\sin(\displaystyle\frac{2\pi k }{N}n)\end{split}\]

For any two complex numbers \(z_1, z_2 \in \mathbb{C}\), \(((z_1+z_2)^*={z_1}^* + {z_2}^*)\). In order to prove this let \(z_1=a_1 + jb_1\) and \(z_2=a_2 + jb_2\).

(9)\[\begin{split}\Rightarrow (z_1 + z_2)^* &=(a_1 + a_2 + j(b_1 + b_2))^* \\ & = a_1+a_2-j(b_1 + b_2)=(a_1-jb_1)+(a_2-jb_2)={z_1}^* + {z_2}^*\end{split}\]

Using this result the complex conjugate of \(x(n)\) can be calculated as

(10)\[{x(n)}^*=\sum_{k=0}^{N-1}\Big\lbrace c_k \cos\Big(\frac{2\pi k}{N}n\Big) + jc_k\sin\Big(\frac{2\pi k }{N}n\Big) \Big\rbrace ^*\]

Let \(c_k=a_k +jb_k\). Then (10) becomes:

(11)\[\begin{split}{x(n)}^* &=\sum_{k=0}^{N-1}\Big\lbrace (a_k + jb_k)\cos\Big(\displaystyle\frac{2\pi k}{N}n\Big) + j(a_k + jb_k)\sin\Big(\displaystyle\frac{2\pi k }{N}n\Big) \Big\rbrace ^* \\ &=\sum _{k=0}^{N-1}\Big\lbrace \Big( a_k\cos\Big(\displaystyle\frac{2\pi k}{N}n \Big) - b_k\sin\Big( \displaystyle\frac{2\pi k}{N}n \Big) \Big)+j\Big(b_k\cos\Big( \displaystyle\frac{2\pi k }{N}n \Big) + a_k\sin\Big( \displaystyle\frac{2\pi k }{N}n \Big) \Big) \Big\rbrace ^* \\ &=\sum_{k=0}^{N-1}\Big(a_k\cos\Big(\displaystyle\frac{2\pi k}{N}n\Big) - b_k\sin\Big(\displaystyle\frac{2\pi k}{N}n\Big)\Big)-j\Big(b_k\cos\Big(\displaystyle\frac{2\pi k}{N}n\Big) +a_k\sin\Big(\displaystyle\frac{2\pi k}{N}n\Big)\Big) \\ &=\sum_{k=0}^{N-1}\Big(a_k -jb_k\Big)\cos\Big(\displaystyle\frac{2\pi k}{N}n\Big)-j\Big(a_k - jb_k\Big)\sin\Big(\displaystyle\frac{2\pi k}{N}n\Big) \\ &=\sum_{k=0}^{N-1}\Big(a_k - jb_k\Big)\Big(\cos\Big(\displaystyle\frac{2\pi k}{N}n\Big)-j\sin\Big(\displaystyle\frac{2\pi k}{N}n\Big)\Big) \\ &=\sum_{k=0}^{N-1}{c_k}^* e^{-j2\pi kn/N}\end{split}\]

Using equation (8), the power of a periodic signal \(x(n)\) can be written as:

\[\begin{split}P_x &= \frac{1}{N}\sum_{n=0}^{N-1}x(n)\sum_{k=0}^{N-1}{c_k}^* e^{-j2\pi kn/N} \\ &=\sum_{k=0}^{N-1}{c_k}^*\frac{1}{N}\sum_{n=0}^{N-1}x(n)e^{-j2\pi kn/N} \\ &=\sum_{k=0}^{N-1}{c_k}^*c_k=\sum_{k=0}^{N-1}|c_k|^2\end{split}\]
